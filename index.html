1. Manhattan Address System

Manhattan is a pocket math trick.

Before everyone had maps in their pocket, New Yorkers used little street-finder cards to estimate the nearest crosstown street for an avenue address.

A

Fifth Avenue splits east from west

Numbered streets use East or West based on which side of Fifth Avenue they are on. Smaller numbers are usually closer to the divider.

B

Street numbers climb north

14th Street is below 23rd, 23rd is below 34th, and the grid keeps marching uptown.

C

Avenue addresses use a formula

The old card trick is: drop the last digit, halve what is left, then add the avenue's key number. It is an estimate, not GPS.

2. Queens Address System

Queens hides a map inside the hyphen.

Queens looks chaotic until the address breaks open. The first number points to the lower-numbered cross street, the second number is the building's spot on the block, and the street name is where the building actually sits.

43 cross street - 12 house number 47th Avenue street name
1

Streets, Places, and Lanes

These usually run north-south and increase as you move east.

2

Avenues, Roads, Drives, and Terraces

These usually run east-west and increase as you move south.

3

Boulevards and old names are wild cards

Queens absorbed older communities, so named roads and big boulevards can bend the rules.
