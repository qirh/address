Part 1

How Manhattan addresses work

Manhattan is easiest above Houston Street: streets mostly run crosstown, avenues mostly run uptown and downtown, and Fifth Avenue is the address divider for east and west streets.

The mental model

  • Numbered streets climb as you go north: 14th, 23rd, 34th, 42nd, 59th, and so on.
  • Fifth Avenue divides most numbered streets into East and West. Smaller street-address numbers are closer to Fifth Avenue.
  • On many crosstown blocks, the first hundred addresses are the first block away from Fifth Avenue, the next hundred is the next block, and so on.
  • For avenue addresses, New Yorkers use a rough calculator: building number divided by 20, plus a street-specific offset.

129 W 81st St

West means west of Fifth Avenue. The 100 block is roughly the second avenue block west of Fifth, so you should think around Columbus or Amsterdam depending on the neighborhood layout.

501 Fifth Ave

The avenue-address trick estimates this near 43rd Street. In real life it sits around 42nd Street, which is why this rule is a guide, not GPS.

10 E 14th St

East means east of Fifth Avenue. A small number like 10 means you are very close to the divider.

Part 2

How Queens addresses work

Queens uses a hyphenated house-number system on most streets. Once you learn the split, an address can give you a walking clue before you even open a map.

The hyphen is the clue

31-19 37th Street
  • The number before the hyphen is a practical wayfinding clue for the nearest numbered cross street or grid line.
  • The number after the hyphen is the building's position on that block.
  • The street name after the house number is the street the building is actually on.
  • The borough has exceptions because older towns and villages were knitted into one Queens map.
  • Official systems still treat the hyphenated value as one house number, so use the split as a navigation shortcut rather than a legal-address rule.

31-19 37th Street

"31" is the nearby-grid clue. "19" is the house number on the block. "37th Street" is the street you are on.

43-12 47th Avenue

The prefix "43" is the nearby-grid clue. The building sits on 47th Avenue, not on 43rd Street.

22-28 36th Street

In Queens, this is one hyphenated house number. It is not the same as a Manhattan-style range like 22-28 Reade Street.
